# Eric Ries

*Author of The Lean Startup and Incorruptible. Founder of the Long-Term Stock Exchange. Source on protecting mission-driven companies from corruption.*

---

## Background

Eric Ries wrote The Lean Startup (2011), one of the most widely-applied business books of the 2010s, which gave a generation of founders the vocabulary for build-measure-learn loops, minimum viable products, and validated learning. His subsequent books are The Startup Way and The Leader's Guide. His next book is Incorruptible: Why Good Companies Go Bad... And How Great Companies Stay Great, published by Authors Equity in May 2026.

He is also the founder of the Long-Term Stock Exchange (LTSE), the first new US stock exchange to both list and trade multiple stocks since the creation of the NASDAQ fifty years ago. LTSE was designed as a structural defense against short-term thinking. Incorruptible grew out of what Ries learned building LTSE and watching one Lean-Startup-influenced company after another get surgically deboned through the IPO process.

## Why he is on this wiki

The Lean Startup taught founders how to build something. Incorruptible teaches founders how to protect what they have built. Both questions matter for an agentic business. Most founders treat the first as the whole job. Ries' core observation, learned the hard way through LTSE and a portfolio of corrupted companies, is that everything worth protecting will eventually need protection, and the protections are an engineering problem, not a willpower problem.

His framework is unusually concrete for a book in this category. He names specific corporate forms, specific governance structures, specific case studies (Sol Price's FedMart, Costco, Patagonia, Vanguard, and dozens more), and gives founders a working blueprint for what he calls mission-controlled organizations: neither investor-controlled nor founder-controlled, structured so the mission survives contact with success.

For the agentic-business audience this matters specifically because AI compresses the timeline from "founded" to "target." A founder building today can scale into capture pressure faster than the founders Ries was originally writing for, with less time to design defenses. Reading the book before designing the next financing round is the difference between sovereignty and surrender.

## How to cite him

For Ries, the source is the published work. When paraphrasing, attribute to "Eric Ries, Incorruptible (Authors Equity, 2026)" or to the specific chapter. For verbatim quotes from the finished book, use a blockquote and cite the chapter. Verbatim quotes from the uncorrected proof should be checked against the final published edition before being published anywhere outside this wiki.
