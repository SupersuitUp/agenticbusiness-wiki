# Gino Wickman

*Creator of the Entrepreneurial Operating System (EOS). Author of Traction. Built EOS into the default operating framework for tens of thousands of growth-stage businesses, run by a worldwide network of certified Implementers.*

---

## Background

Gino Wickman developed the Entrepreneurial Operating System (EOS) over two decades of running and turning around small and mid-sized businesses. He codified the system in *Traction: Get a Grip on Your Business* (2007), now the canonical operating manual for businesses in the roughly 10 to 250 employee range. Companion books, *Rocket Fuel*, *How to be a Great Boss*, and *Get a Grip*, expand the framework into hiring, leadership pairing, and implementation.

EOS today is run by a worldwide network of certified Implementers who help leadership teams install the system: the Accountability Chart, the Vision/Traction Organizer, the Scorecard, the Level 10 Meeting, the Rocks, the IDS (Identify, Discuss, Solve) discipline, and the GWC (Gets it, Wants it, Capacity) hiring filter. The franchise model means the same toolkit shows up across thousands of companies with consistent outcomes.

## Why he is on this wiki

Most writing on organizational structure treats it as something every founder has to invent from first principles. It is not. There is a small number of proven core operating systems for a growing business, and EOS is the one this wiki recommends as the default.

The reason is mundane and important. Most of the operational pain in a mid-stage company is structural, not strategic. People do not know who owns what. The leadership team meets without a format. Decisions stall because there is no IDS discipline. New hires do not stick because there is no GWC filter. EOS resolves all of these with a coherent, battle-tested kit that thousands of businesses have already proven works. A founder who installs EOS gets back the months of organizational debugging they would otherwise spend inventing a worse version of the same thing.

The principles contributed by Gino to this wiki are not the whole of EOS. They are the specific load-bearing rules that apply directly to operating an agentic business, drawn out of EOS and recontextualized for the operator who is also wiring AI into every seat.

## Principles contributed to this wiki

- **[Five Bullets Per Seat](/principles/five-bullets-per-seat)**: every seat is defined by no more than five outcome-bullets, what the person is responsible for. The mechanisms they use to deliver those outcomes are theirs to figure out.
- **[GWC the Seat](/principles/gwc-the-seat)**: every seat-holder must Get it, Want it, and have the Capacity to do it. Three yes-or-no questions. A "no" on any one means the seat is wrong for the person, full stop.
- **[The Level 10 Meeting](/principles/the-level-10-meeting)**: the leadership team meets weekly, same day, same time, same one-page agenda. Ninety minutes. Sixty of those minutes belong to solving issues.
- **[IDS the Issue](/principles/ids-the-issue)**: most teams discuss endlessly, identify rarely, and solve almost nothing. Reverse the ratio. The stated problem is almost never the real one. Identify, Discuss, Solve.

More EOS-distilled principles are planned, including Rocks (the 90-day priority discipline) and the V/TO (Vision/Traction Organizer).

## How to cite him

The canonical source is *Traction: Get a Grip on Your Business* (2007). For specific teachings, either the EOS books or a named EOS Implementer's public material is an acceptable source. The author's name and the book title are part of the citation.

When a public-teaching source from a certified Implementer is used, attribute the Implementer in the Field Notes log on this page, then attribute the underlying principle to Gino and EOS on the principle page itself.

## Field Notes

A log of specific EOS sources this wiki has lifted from.

### 2021-08-10, "Strengthening the Accountability Chart" by Rick Fawcett (YouTube)

Rick Fawcett, an Expert EOS Implementer, walks through how to harden the Accountability Chart so it actually drives accountability across the organization. The video opens with the Gino quote that the root of all evil in an organization is in the accountability chart, then teaches three steps (define functions, define roles, put the right name in the seat), the five-bullet discipline for compressing seats, the GWC filter for evaluating the seat-holder, and two red flags (too many bullets, too many words per bullet).

> "The root of all evil in an organization lies in the accountability chart."
>
> Gino Wickman, paraphrased by Rick Fawcett, [Strengthening the Accountability Chart](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=T_ob72LrCp0), 2021
