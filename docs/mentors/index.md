# Mentors

*Who the wisdom on this wiki comes from. Some mentors shared principles in private conversation, anonymized here as archetypes. Others are sourced via their published books or public teaching content and cited by their real names.*

---

The wisdom on this wiki is sourced from specific people who have lived the answers to specific questions. There is no generic advice and no podcast clips harvested at random. Every page traces back to a source category named below.

Each mentor page documents what the person or archetype has operated at a level that earns them a voice on this wiki, which principles trace back to them, and the source category they fall into.

## Source categories

Three buckets, in order of attribution privacy.

**Private-conversation mentors, anonymized as archetypes.** Operators who shared their principles in real conversations with the wiki's stewards. Their wisdom is distilled here under archetype names that capture their operating posture without identifying them. Personal details, organizations, geographies, and biographical specifics are kept off the wiki. The conversations are real and the dates are recorded.

**Published-book mentors, cited by author.** Authors whose published books are the source. The book is public, the author's name is part of the citation, and the mentor page summarizes the relevant arc of the work and links out to it. No quoting from podcasts the author appeared on. The book is the source.

**Public-teaching mentors, cited by name.** Operators whose own public teaching content (videos, training programs, and operator-facing material under their own brand) is the source. Their teaching is already public; the citation matches.

## How mentors get added

A mentor lands on this wiki when three things are true. They have built or operated a business at a level that produces wisdom most operators do not have access to. They fit one of the three source categories above. They have consented to being included (or, in the published-source cases, their work in public is the consent).

The wiki is selective on purpose. Adding a mentor is a credibility statement.

Now runs a portfolio of businesses across regulated and high-information industries. Source of the foundational principles around magic moments, minimum proofpoints, niche domination, and chips-not-cash.
- **[Eric Ries](/mentors/eric-ries)** *(published-book source)*: author of The Lean Startup and Incorruptible, founder of the Long-Term Stock Exchange. Source on protecting mission-driven companies from corruption (governance, ownership, mission lock-in).
- **[Sharran Srivatsaa](/mentors/sharran-srivatsaa)** *(public-teaching source)*: CEO of Acquisition.com. Scaled Teles Properties 10x in five years to $3.4 billion and sold to Douglas Elliman. Source on hiring mechanics and A-player attraction.
