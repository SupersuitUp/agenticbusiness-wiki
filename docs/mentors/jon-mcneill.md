# Jon McNeill

*Former President of Tesla. Built and exited five operating companies. Author of The Algorithm. Source on order-of-magnitude goal setting, go-and-see operating, and three-sentence executive communication.*

---

## Background

McNeill served as President of Tesla from 2015 to 2018, the period in which the company scaled from roughly four thousand to forty thousand employees, launched the Model 3 and Model X at volume, opened more than 250 retail stores worldwide, and rebuilt its online sales engine. Before Tesla he founded and exited five operating companies across collision repair, software, real estate services, and cyber security. He is currently a venture investor and operator, and the author of *The Algorithm*, a book documenting the operating framework he and Elon Musk used to push decision-making to the edge at Tesla.

He grew up in the back of his grandfather's auto repair shop, started his career writing trading algorithms on early neural nets in the late 1990s, and has built a career out of moving between industries while applying a small set of repeatable operating principles.

## Why he is on this wiki

Tesla in the McNeill era is one of the cleanest available case studies of an operator-driven scale-up that did not rely on the founder being in every room. McNeill's job, by his own description, was to build the framework that let frontline people at Tesla solve problems faster than the senior management team could have. The framework is mostly a small set of disciplines that are useful to every operator running an agentic business, regardless of industry.

His contribution to this wiki is the discipline of setting goals too big to be solved by tweaking the status quo, of using direct observation as the leader's primary analytical instrument, and of demanding extreme efficiency in how information moves through an organization. All three are amplified, not replaced, when agents become first-class workers.

He is also a useful counter-example to the "lone genius" framing of Elon Musk's success. The framework, in his telling, is what scaled. The frontline people, equipped with the framework, are what built the company.

## Principles contributed to this wiki

- **[Order of Magnitude Goal Setting](/principles/order-of-magnitude-goal-setting)**: 10x and 100x goals force people to question the business model itself. Tweaks deliver three to five percent. Real reinvention starts at 10x.
- **[Go and See, Two Eyes Two Ears](/principles/go-and-see)**: the leader's most powerful analytical instrument is direct observation of the work. Mystery-shop the funnel, walk the line where inventory stacks, watch real customers struggle with the real product. The insight arrives faster than any dashboard can deliver it.
- **[Three Sentence Discipline](/principles/three-sentence-discipline)**: the CEO's most precious commodity is information processing time. Every escalation collapses to three sentences: what is the problem, what is the root cause, what is the proposed solution. The discipline sharpens the sender's thinking as much as it conserves the receiver's time.

More principles may be distilled from his ongoing public teaching and from *The Algorithm* over time.

## Field Notes

### 2026-04-09 — "Ex-Tesla President reveals EVERYTHING Elon does to win" (Podcast)

Hour-long appearance on the My First Million podcast (Sam Parr and Shaan Puri). McNeill walked through the operating framework he calls the algorithm, with three principles distilled to wiki pages: order-of-magnitude goal setting (the 20x online-sales mandate that collapsed Tesla's car configurator from 360,000 combinations to two), go-and-see operating (the Falcon Wing door bottleneck solved by a maintenance jig after ten minutes of standing at the assembly point, lineage traced back to Sam Walton's secret-shop habit and Scott Cook's follow-me-home), and the three-sentence email Elon used to run the company.

Other useful threads not yet on the wiki: cycle-time versus touch-time as a market-opening lens (the eighteen-day collision-repair vs six-to-eight-hour touch insight that became Service King), one-size-fits-all as an opportunity signal (the cyber-platform-for-SMB thesis), the value of staying in everyday consumer friction (flying commercial as a source of business ideas), and the framing of AI as exoskeleton rather than replacement.

> "Part of the secret to Elon's secret sauce is he sets these order of magnitude improvement goals. So 10x, 100x. He came to me and said, 'Hey, look, we got to figure out how to sell cars online.' Cuz nobody was buying $120,000 things sight unseen online. And so he turns to me. He says, 'This is your goal. Improve digital sales by 20x.' So now it's like, 'Oh crap, I got to think way differently about this.'"
>
> — Jon McNeill, [Ex-Tesla President reveals EVERYTHING Elon does to win](https://youtu.be/GG4TwQEYdBY), My First Million, 2026-04-09
