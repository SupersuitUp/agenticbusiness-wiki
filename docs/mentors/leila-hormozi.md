# Leila Hormozi

*Co-founder and CEO of Acquisition.com, the $250M+/year portfolio she runs with Alex Hormozi. Source on operator discipline, management, and the human-execution layer the agentic stack sits on top of.*

---

## Background

Leila Hormozi co-founded Acquisition.com with Alex Hormozi and serves as its CEO. Acquisition.com is the family office through which they operate and invest in a portfolio of companies producing $250M+ in annual revenue.

Before Acquisition.com, she built and exited operating companies alongside Alex, Gym Launch and Prestige Labs among them, sold in a combined deal at a $46.2M valuation. Her lane in the partnership is the operating side: people, management, culture, and the systems that let a business run without the founder in every decision.

She tells her own origin plainly. Arrested six times by 19, broke and lost. First million by 23. Now 33, she publishes operator-facing content on management and leadership through her YouTube channel and the Build with Leila Hormozi podcast, aimed at people building and running companies.

## Why she is on this wiki

An agentic business is a stack of systems, but the stack sits on top of one irreducible layer: the operator's own judgment and discipline. Agents handle the infinite reactive surface. The hard, ambiguous, highest-leverage work stays human, and it gets done well only if the operator stays sharp enough to do it.

Hormozi's contribution is that human layer. Her teaching is about the habits that keep an operator capable of the undelegatable work: delaying gratification, facing the numbers, taking radical responsibility, and doing the hardest thing first while cognition is strongest. The agentic stack multiplies an operator who has these habits. It does nothing for one who does not.

She is also precise about decision quality as a perishable resource, which is the exact constraint that makes the eat-the-frog discipline matter for someone running a business on agents.

## Principles contributed to this wiki

- **[Eat the Frog First](/principles/eat-the-frog-first)**: do your hardest, highest-leverage task first thing, before the reactive surface of inbox, chat, and agent-review queues consumes your sharpest hours. Decision quality is perishable, so spend peak cognition on the one task that needs it.

More principles may be distilled from her public teaching over time. Her six-habits material alone carries several candidates: facing the numbers daily, radical responsibility, skills over status symbols, and manufacturing discomfort. The YouTube channel and the Build with Leila Hormozi podcast are the primary sources.

## Field Notes

A dated log of what was lifted from each Hormozi piece this wiki has processed. Most recent first.

### 2025-11-21 — "I'm 33. If You're In Your 20s or 30s, Watch This." (YouTube)

A fifteen-minute video laying out six habits for building wealth, framed as habits that have nothing to do with money and everything to do with trading comfort for growth. Habit four, do the hard things first, is the one lifted here. Hormozi cites Brian Tracy's "eat the frog": the hardest, highest-leverage task gets done first thing in the morning, before email or any feed, under the rule that nothing else is allowed until it is done. She pairs it with her own discipline of guarding decision quality by time of day, refusing hard calls past 4 or 5 p.m. because the brain is no longer sharp.

> "Success belongs to people who are willing to swallow the hardest bite before anything else."
>
> Leila Hormozi, [I'm 33. If You're In Your 20s or 30s, Watch This.](https://youtu.be/Kg2Ux47hwg4), YouTube, 2025-11-21

Landed on the wiki as [Eat the Frog First](/principles/eat-the-frog-first).
