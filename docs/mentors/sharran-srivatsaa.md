# Sharran Srivatsaa

*CEO of Acquisition.com. Scaled Teles Properties 10x in five years to $3.4 billion and sold to Douglas Elliman. Five exits in nineteen years. Source on hiring, operating cadence, and the mechanics of A-player attraction.*

## Background

Sharran Srivatsaa is the CEO of Acquisition.com, the holding company founded by Alex and Leila Hormozi. He stepped into the CEO role in early 2026 after serving as President from June 2025, as Leila Hormozi moved into the chairwoman seat.

Before Acquisition.com, he was President of Real (NASDAQ: REAX), one of the fastest-growing publicly traded real estate brokerages in the world. Earlier, he scaled Teles Properties 10x in five years to $3.4 billion in sales and sold the business to Douglas Elliman (NYSE: DOUG). He started his career at Goldman Sachs and Credit Suisse, working with management teams of fast-growing companies on strategic planning and corporate finance. He is a four-time Inc. 500 entrepreneur with five exits over nineteen years.

Bachelor's degree in computer science and mathematics from Luther College, Academic All-American. Competed on the professional tennis tour. Worked in Silicon Valley with startup companies and internationally at Four Seasons and Ritz-Carlton resorts.

## Why he is on this wiki

Sharran teaches the operating mechanics that compound an enterprise: hiring, weekly cadence, communication standards, and the work of attracting talent who can carry their own seat. He has lived through multiple exits at the level where the wrong hire is a seven-figure mistake and the right hire changes the company's trajectory. He has also taught operators in this lane long enough to have crisp, repeatable formulations of principles that survive across industries.

For this wiki specifically, his contribution is the mechanic that converts raw operator pain and raw operator aspiration into the job description that attracts the A-player who matches your specific situation. The principle reframes the entire hiring conversation away from "where do we find A-players" toward "have we articulated the seat clearly enough for the right A-player to see themselves in it."
