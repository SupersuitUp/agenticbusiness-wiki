# Customers Sniff the Slop

*Customers can tell when an operator puts less effort into them. AI used without extreme coaching ships sloppy generic work. AI used to replace warm human touchpoints ships sloppy fake relationships. Coach the AI extremely where it draws; protect the human where the relationship lives.*

## What it is

A discipline for principals deploying AI inside a business that already has a customer relationship. The customers who were loyal to the business before AI know what the relationship used to feel like. They will notice, instantly, when the relationship downgrades. The two ways AI commonly downgrades a customer relationship are slop quality and slop warmth, and the principle prevents both.

Slop quality is what AI produces when it has not been coached extremely on what differentiated work looks like inside the specific business. Slop warmth is what happens when an automated process replaces a touchpoint the customer used to associate with a human who cared. Both are detectable. Both predict churn.

## Extreme coaching is the price of differentiated AI output

Default AI output collapses to the [propaganda-weighted average](https://appliedai.wiki/concepts/propaganda-weighted-average) of the public internet. That average is structurally promotional, structurally generic, and structurally wrong about the specific things a specific customer in a specific subculture cares about.

The differentiator in most businesses is exactly the layer the public internet does not document well. The exact model of bag the in-the-know subculture is actually carrying this season. The brand the cohort is about to make popular, two cycles before it indexes online. The phrasing only a certain kind of customer responds to. The internal knowledge of what "quality" means in this specific function for this specific company. That insider-grade specificity is what extreme coaching loads into the briefing.

Without it, the AI's output reads to the customer like a slightly off-tonal alien who has read about humans but never met one. Customers stop responding without articulating why. The answer is that the output started to feel generic where it used to feel known.

The threshold is not "well-prompted." It is *extremely* coached. Ruthlessly clear about who the customer is, what they care about, what differentiated quality looks like, and what is true about this specific situation that the model cannot drift to the average. Anything less ships slop in proportion to how soft the coaching was. The companies riding high revenue from AI infrastructure today are eating churn underneath that revenue, because their customers do not know how to coach their AI past slop.

## The human touchpoint is where customers feel the love

The other failure mode is structurally different and equally fatal. It is replacing the human warmth in a relationship the customer already had.

A customer who used to talk to a sales rep who knew them, who got a thoughtful follow-up email that felt like one specific human writing to them, who called and was recognized by name, will sniff the substitution the moment an automated process takes over the touchpoint. The substitution does not need to be a bad bot. Even a competent bot is a downgrade if the customer was used to a human. The signal the customer receives is "this business is putting less effort and love into our relationship." That signal predicts churn at a rate the cost savings from the automation will not cover.

The discipline is to keep the human-good-with-people on the human-good-with-people work. The complementary discipline is to free that person from administrative work so they can spend more time on the relationship, not less. The AI does the admin. The human does the warmth. Swap those two roles and the business loses what it was already paying for.

## What the principle looks like in practice

Three operating moves.

**Identify which touchpoints are relationship-load-bearing.** Sales calls with named accounts. Onboarding for high-touch customers. The follow-up email after a hard conversation. The thank-you note that used to feel hand-written. These are the touchpoints the customer associates with a specific human who cared. They get human treatment, full stop. No automation in the layer the customer touches.

**Where AI does customer-facing work, coach it extremely.** Briefings approach specification grade. Success criteria are spelled out. Ground-truth context about the specific customer and the specific subculture is loaded explicitly. The grading bar is "would this be indistinguishable from the best version of this work the business has ever shipped." Anything below that ships slop, even if the slop sounds confident.

**Use AI to expand human capacity for the warm work.** The right deployment of AI in a customer-facing business is on the administrative layer behind the warm touchpoints. Calendaring. Summarization. Drafting first passes the human edits. Prep dossiers before customer calls. Internal status updates. The hours that used to get eaten by admin become hours available for the relationship.

## What good looks like

An operator running this principle can:

- Name the three to five customer touchpoints where the business's relationship lives, and confirm a specific named human handles each
- Quote a customer who said, unprompted, that the business's communication is "specifically right" for their world
- Point to one AI-driven workflow where the briefing reads like a specification and the output is indistinguishable from the operator's best human work
- Demonstrate that the company's best human-relationship operators are spending more time with customers this quarter than last, not less

If any of those four are missing, the business is leaking customers in ways the dashboard will report only after the churn has already happened.

## Common mistakes

- **Treating "coachable" as a gentle slope.** It is a cliff. With light coaching the AI defaults to the propaganda-weighted average, which is generic, which customers sniff. The threshold for usefulness is extreme coaching, not reasonable coaching.
- **Replacing the human in a touchpoint the customer associated with a relationship.** Even a competent bot is a downgrade. The cost savings does not cover the churn signal it sends.
- **Believing the demo translates to production.** Demo briefings are tight. Production briefings are one-line prompts. The customer-facing output diverges fast under that gap.
- **Using AI for the relationship work and asking the human to do the admin.** That is the inversion of the right configuration. The human is the substrate of the warmth; the AI is the substrate of the leverage.
