# Leverage and Chokepoints

*Every industry has a structure. Be the chokepoint or build the workaround. Never operate without a point of leverage.*

---

*Articulated by [Michael B. Ajouz](/mentors/mike-ajouz) in conversation, 2026-05-15.*

## What it is

In every industry, value concentrates at specific structural positions: the platforms, the gatekeepers, the distribution chokepoints, the rights holders. These are where leverage lives. Operating without one of those positions, or without a credible workaround that disintermediates one, means you are at the mercy of whoever has it.

The principle: do not build a business without explicit clarity about your point of leverage. Either you ARE the chokepoint, or you have a workaround that bypasses an existing one.

> "You're either the checkpoint, which is the historical game, or the workaround, which is the new game. But your industry structure and who has leverage is what determines whether you'll make money or not, or how easy it will be. So you always need points of leverage, always."
>
> — Michael B. Ajouz, 2026-05-15

## Why it matters

A business without leverage trades labor for cash and hopes the trade keeps working. A business with leverage extracts a structural rent. Over time, the leveraged business compounds. The unlevered business stays where it is until something changes that takes the business out.

This is the difference between owning the venue and selling tickets to it. The venue owner captures the structural value. The ticket seller captures the labor.

For agentic businesses specifically: AI is creating new chokepoints (model providers, agent harness providers, distribution platforms) and dissolving old ones (Google's search monopoly, traditional content platforms). Founders building now have an unusual window to either become the new chokepoint or to build the workaround that disintermediates the old one.

## How to find your leverage

Three diagnostic questions.

**Who has the chokepoint in your industry?** Name them. The platforms, the distribution networks, the rights holders, the trusted intermediaries. If you cannot name them, you do not understand the industry structure yet.

**Are you them, or are you working around them?** Pick. The two strategies require different capital structures, different teams, different time horizons. Hybrid is usually a tell that the leverage has not been thought through.

**What happens if the chokepoint changes its rules?** A business that is renting access to someone else's chokepoint can be killed by a policy change. A business that is the chokepoint or has a real workaround survives policy changes. Stress-test the position.

## Worked examples

From the conversation:

**Music industry.** The chokepoint is distribution and rights ownership (major labels, streaming platforms). Operating as an independent artist without leverage means trading talent for tiny revenue shares. The workaround is owning the platform that artists need (a game, a media outlet, a community). Once you have the workaround, you have leverage in your negotiations with everyone else in the value chain.

**Insurance.** The chokepoint is the underwriting platform. The dinosaurs (the established carriers) own the platform and rent out the access. Ajouz's strategy: build a competing platform from scratch and bring it in-house. Once the platform is yours, the structural rent flips from going to the dinosaurs to going to you.

**Real estate.** The chokepoint is access to deals. Owning the relationships and the underwriting capability is the chokepoint. Pure consulting without ownership of the deal pipeline is leverageless.

**Concert promotion.** Live Nation owns the venue chokepoint and captures concession value. Artists who sell tickets and merchandise but do not own venues or distribution are working without leverage.

## What good looks like

A founder operating with a clear leverage position can:

- Name the chokepoint in their industry
- State whether they are the chokepoint or have a specific workaround
- Describe the path to defending the position from new entrants
- Explain why their position will be more valuable in five years than today

If the founder cannot answer those four, the leverage is not real and the business is at risk of being commoditized by anyone with a similar offering plus capital.

## Common mistakes

- **Operating without leverage and not noticing.** A founder building a high-quality product without a structural position will get out-competed by an inferior product that has one. Quality alone is not leverage.
- **Treating "best in class" as leverage.** Being better than competitors is not the same as having a structural rent. The structural rent comes from the position, not the quality of the offering.
- **Confusing partnerships with leverage.** A partnership with a chokepoint owner can be revoked. Leverage is owning the position, not having access to it.
- **Picking the workaround when the chokepoint is the right play.** If you can credibly become the chokepoint, do that. The workaround is the right play when the chokepoint is structurally owned by someone you cannot displace.
