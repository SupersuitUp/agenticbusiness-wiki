# What is Applied AI

*Putting AI to its highest and best use in service of multiplying a business's potential. Not a chatbot. Not headcount reduction. A deliberate, human-led deployment that keeps the irreplaceable human elements intact.*

---

## What it is not

Applied AI is not a chatbot. It is not a robot. It is not automation as an endpoint. It is not doing more of what you are already doing, faster, regardless of whether the work was worth doing in the first place.

Each of those framings is real. None of them is the thing.

## What it is

Applied AI is putting AI to its **highest and best use** in service of the work your business is actually called to.

The definition that travels:

> **Applied AI is the deliberate, human-led deployment of AI so that the business can deliver value at a scale that was previously impossible, while keeping the irreplaceably human elements of the work intact.**

The operative word is *applied*. Not studied. Not speculated about. Not treated as a vendor subscription. Pointed at a goal and deployed.

## The two schools

Two schools of thought have emerged around AI in businesses. They are not two points on the same spectrum. They are fundamentally different philosophies with fundamentally different outcomes.

**Reductive cost-cutting.** AI as a mechanism to replace headcount, automate tasks, and reduce expenses, with the savings as the end goal. The operative question: *how many people can we replace?*

**Generative value-creation.** AI to amplify human capacity, unlock new capabilities, and deliver impact at a scale that was previously impossible, with the mission as the end goal. The operative question: *what can our people become?*

Any well-run business using AI eliminates unnecessary redundancies. That is operational hygiene. The distinction between the two schools is what drives the strategy. Reductive treats headcount reduction as the destination. Generative treats it as a byproduct of building something better.

The reductive approach has already hit its floor. Klarna replaced 700 employees with AI, watched quality collapse, and had to reverse course. The reductive school treats people as line items. Applied AI treats people as irreplaceable assets to be amplified. **The businesses that define the next decade are not the ones that cut the most. They are the ones that create the most value with the humans they have.**

## What an agentic business is

An **agentic business** is one that runs on applied AI. The founder sets direction. AI systems handle the operations that can be handed off. The humans in the system do the work that only humans can do.

- Four hours of customer feedback synthesis collapses to four minutes.
- Every document starts at seventy percent instead of zero.
- Skills gaps get mapped in real time rather than once a year.
- Strategic blockers get named, interviewed, and turned into artifacts in fifteen minutes instead of fifteen hours.

In each case, the human still makes the call. The human is just making better calls, faster, with more information.

This is the floor, not the ceiling. The businesses furthest along are building self-improving operational systems: AI that monitors its own output, identifies what is working, and adjusts without being told to. The founder's job shifts from running the machine to upgrading the machine.

## Two discipline principles

**Think in eighty-twenty.** Applied AI amplifies whatever you point it at. The discipline is asking, on every problem: what are the twenty percent of moves that unlock eighty percent of the leverage here? Most of what you could do with AI is not worth doing. The game is picking the few moves that are.

**Do not scale what is not working.** AI accelerates volume. It does not fix a broken strategy. If a program is not landing, if a pitch is not converting, if a relationship is not deepening, applied AI makes the business do more of what is not working, faster. The framework comes first. The tools serve the framework.
